2. Why Arrow Is Doing This & Why Now
Why Arrow Is Doing This
  • Margin compression is severe — operating margin collapsed from 5.59% (2022) to 2.39% (2025 Q3 YTD); EBITDA eroded from $2.26B to sub-$1B; SG&A rose from 6.93% to 7.92% of revenue[BRIEFING]
  • SG&A is structurally too heavy — at ~$2.2B annually, unsustainable for a thin-margin distribution model at current revenue levels[BRIEFING]
  • Cost cuts alone won't suffice — Arrow needs global process standardization + AI-enabled automation for structural efficiency, not just labor arbitrage[RFP §1.1]
  • Fragmented supplier landscape — 360+ FTE across multiple incumbents in Bangalore, Mysore, Puerto Rico, and Dalian need consolidation under one partner[RFP §1.3]
  • Customer experience gaps — both Global Components & ECS business units need better service delivery to channel partners[RFP §1.1]
Why Now?
  • $185M Bain restructuring ending 2026 — committed to $100M/yr run-rate savings; this outsourcing deal is the next transformation wave[BRIEFING]
  • CEO transition + IT spec freeze — Sean Kerins separated Sep 2025; Bill Austen (interim) drives decisive cost action; 18-month IT freeze has compounded digital debt[BRIEFING]
  • Credit rating under pressure — BBB- with 2.7x leverage; must demonstrate cost discipline to maintain investment-grade status[BRIEFING]
  • Revenue declined ~25% from $37.1B (2022) to $27.9B (2024) — SG&A hasn't adjusted proportionally, making the cost base untenable[BRIEFING]
  • AI inflection point — agentic AI, intelligent document processing, and predictive analytics can now deliver step-change SG&A productivity[RFP §1.1]
  • Hackett Group advisory — rigorous benchmarking signals Arrow is coached to extract maximum value from this engagement[BRIEFING]

About Arrow Electronics
The Invisible Backbone of Global Technology — A Fortune #154 global technology solutions provider, distributing electronic components and enterprise computing solutions across 85+ countries. Founded in 1935, Arrow has grown from a small radio parts retailer on Manhattan's Radio Row into a ~$30B revenue company connecting innovation across the full technology value chain.[1][2]
The Arrow Story: Every smartphone, electric vehicle, medical device, and data center exists because of an invisible network orchestrating millions of components from thousands of suppliers. Arrow Electronics is that network — the world's largest electronic components distributor, connecting 175,000+ suppliers to Fortune 500 OEMs like Tesla, Apple, and Microsoft.

When an automotive OEM designs a next-generation EV platform with 3,000+ components, Arrow orchestrates supply from Analog Devices, Texas Instruments, NXP, and hundreds of smaller manufacturers — managing allocation, compliance, and logistics across continents. When enterprises need cloud and security software, they procure through Arrow's ECS channel. When factories need IoT monitoring systems, Arrow Intelligent Solutions (AIS) designs and assembles them. This is technology's supply chain backbone — a ~$30B operation spanning 90+ warehouses, free trade zones, and real-time logistics intelligence.

AI Philosophy — 7 Guiding Principles
AI within GBS.NEXT is a business transformation lever, not a technology overlay. Sustainable value from AI is realized only when it is embedded into execution, governed responsibly, and absorbed by the operating model.
1. Stabilize Before Intelligence
AI introduced only after execution is stabilized and core processes are standardized and data-ready. AI augments a strong foundation rather than amplifying variability.
2. Outcome-Led, Not Use-Case Driven
AI adoption anchored to business outcomes — margin protection, working capital, decision velocity, CX — rather than isolated experiments.
3. Human-in-the-Loop by Design
AI augments judgment, not replaces accountability. Human oversight at all critical decision points for explainability, trust, and regulatory alignment.
4. Embedded, Not Bolted On
AI capabilities natively integrated into day-to-day workflows, decision forums, and governance — ensuring adoption at scale, not parallel programs.
5. Sequenced & Scalable
Phased roadmap: assisted intelligence → predictive insights → agent-led execution. Arrow scales AI safely as maturity increases.
6. Responsible & Measurable
All AI use governed through defined controls, telemetry, and performance monitoring with clear metrics for impact, adoption, and risk.
7. Enablement Over Dependence
AI literacy enabled across roles so teams can confidently work alongside AI, interpret insights, and intervene when needed. Long-term resilience, not tool dependency.

Solution Theme 3 — Innovation & Revenue Amplification
Enabling, Amplifying, and Generating New Value for Arrow
Enabling
Enabling Arrow’s business units to talk to the market with new capabilities. Arrow doesn’t just get efficiency — they get offerings they couldn’t deliver before.
Amplifying
Amplifying existing strengths — Arrow’s distribution network, supplier relationships, and market position become force-multiplied through AI and analytics.
Generating
Generating new revenue streams — GTM upselling at $50M/yr trajectory. Joint go-to-market. Innovation fund. Ecosystem-led offerings that create value beyond service delivery.
Core Philosophical Principle — Empathy: Acceptance from the lowest common denominator. Our solution must be understood and embraced at every level of Arrow’s organization — from BU Presidents to the operations analysts. If the person doing the work every day doesn’t feel this makes their life better, we haven’t designed it right.

Architect’s Take: Three solution themes, three clean commitments. Zero-Risk Transition answers Arrow’s fear (will this break things?), Agentification answers their aspiration (will this transform us?), and Innovation answers their ambition (will this grow us?). The FTE model tells a seven-year story: 2,800→2,800 on Day 1 (1:1 takeover), ~2,000 by Y1 post lock-in (~30% productivity), ~1,400 by Y2 (~50%), ~840 by Y3–Y5 (~70%), and 560–840 by Y7 (70–80% productivity) — all through agentic orchestration. The $100M upfront isn’t a sweetener — it’s a structural signal: we’re building this together.
